## 📝 Quick Summary
This paper introduces GATSY, a framework that models artist similarity using Graph Attention Networks (GNNs). Instead of relying on LLMs to "guess" if two bands or genres are related, it treats artists and genres as interconnected nodes on a graph. The system formalizes artist and genre similarity as a "Link Prediction task" – essentially calculating the mathematical probability that an edge exists between two entities based on their shared metadata and graph topology.

## 🎯 Key Findings & Metrics
* **Core Mechanism:** They incorporate music genre information directly into the artist's mathematical representation (embedding), enabling "genre-aware similarity."
* **Main result:** Modeling artists and genres in a graph allows the system to effectively link artists even if explicit connections (or exact genre text matches) are missing from the raw data.
* **Why it works without LLMs:** It uses pure matrix multiplication and attention scores to calculate the distance between nodes, completely bypassing the need for generative AI.
